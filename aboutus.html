What we value

Community

Concussions can significantly impact communities by affecting individual well-being, relationships, and participation in daily activities. This can lead to reduced social engagement, isolation, and challenges in school or work. Additionally, concussions can exacerbate existing mental health concerns and increase the risk of long-term health issues.

Saftey

Concussions can trigger or worsen symptoms of anxiety, depression, and other mental health concerns, impacting emotional well-being and potentially leading to substance abuse or even suicide. While rare, there is an elevated risk of suicide in individuals who have suffered concussions, particularly those with repeated injuries.

prevention

Concussion prevention involves a multi-faceted approach, including wearing appropriate protective gear, adhering to sports rules, implementing proper training techniques, and fostering a culture of safety and awareness. It's also crucial to prioritize injury recognition and management, ensuring prompt removal from play and seeking medical evaluation when necessary. Key Strategies for Concussion Prevention: Protective Gear: Wear appropriate helmets and other protective equipment for the specific activity, ensuring proper fit and consistent use. Replace damaged or worn-out equipment. Consider the use of mouthguards, especially in contact sports.

How does it affect you and your health?

severity of concussions

Grade 1

Grade 1 (Mild Concussion): Symptoms are generally mild and short-lived, lasting less than 15 minutes. No loss of consciousness. Symptoms may include headache, dizziness, confusion, and difficulty concentrating.

Grade 2

Grade 2 (Moderate Concussion): Symptoms are more persistent and severe than Grade 1, lasting longer than 15 minutes. May include brief loss of consciousness (less than 5 minutes). Symptoms may include persistent headache, amnesia, ringing in the ears, and irritability.

Grade 3

Grade 3 (Severe Concussion): The most severe type of concussion. Characterized by a loss of consciousness for 5 minutes or more. May include amnesia lasting 24 hours or more. Symptoms may also include vomiting, seeing stars, and difficulty speaking
